Coca-Cola

Working together for a more water-secure future

The Coca-Cola Company and WWF launched a transformational partnership in 2007 to help conserve and protect the world’s freshwater resources. The work has led to major conservation gains, including helping to improve the ecological health of eleven of the world’s most important freshwater basins, increasing the company’s water ef ciency by over 21 per cent, working to prevent 5 million metric tons of CO2 emissions across Coca-Cola’s global manufacturing operations, and promoting more sustainable agricultural practices in its supply chain.

The partners also collaborate on an awareness and fundraising cause marketing campaign “Arctic Home” in North America and Europe.

The collaboration was renewed through to 2020 with a focus on improving the ecological health and resilience of eleven key freshwater basins, improving environmental performance across the company’s value chain, integrating the value of nature into public and private decision-making, and convening in uential partners to solve global environmental challenges.

H&M

Pioneering water stewardship for fashion

WWF and H&M are working together to raise awareness and improve responsible water use throughout the whole value chain – ensuring H&M sets new standards for water stewardship in the fashion industry and inspiring others to follow. H&M’s water strategy is developed in line with WWF’s water stewardship steps.

Supplier waste water is checked against quality levels de ned by the Business for Social Sustainability (BSR) Water Group, as well as improved routines for data veri cation and water testing.

Plans for engaging stakeholders in collective action have been developed in key river basins in China and Bangladesh advocating sustainable water management to meet the demands of ecosystems, communities and businesses.

In 2013-2014, 100 per cent of H&M employees received training around water issues, while key business functions received in-depth training.

Unicredit

Working together on an Environmental Sustainability Programme (ESP)

Since 2009, UniCredit and WWF have cooperated on the ESP which focuses on the challenge of integrating environmental sustainability issues into business strategy and operation. Goals include:

  • Developing a climate strategy that reduces internal emissions (30 per cent reduction in CO2 emissions generated by the bank’s of ces and branches by 2020) and nanced emissions (testing new methodologies to assess emissions associated to the nancing activities of the group)
  • Supporting policy development for environmentally sensitive topics
  • Promoting nancial products and services (to families and companies) for energy ef ciency and renewable sources
  • Raising staff awareness around climate and sustainability
